Call for papers

Papers (of 20 minutes) addressing any of the potential problems facing music as an academic subject within higher education are welcome. Although this conference is a response to policy shifts within the United Kingdom, we would like to receive submissions from our international colleagues; in particular, we hope to be in a position to share examples of how music has thrived or suffered within other higher education systems and any exemplary practices that have developed as a response.

Abstracts (of no more than 300 words) should be emailed to mail@senzasordino.org by Friday 29 June.

Call for performances

Performances that demonstrate current elements within music research and practice are also welcome. Proposals (of no more than 300 words) with links to examples of your work should be emailed to mail@senzasordino.org by Friday 29 June.
